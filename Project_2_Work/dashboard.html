1. Planting Foot

The plant foot should be placed right about even with the ball.

2. Ankle

The kicking foot should swing through and contact the ball with a locked ankle ankle.

3. Follow Through

Focus on a solid strong back swing and a smooth and natural forwards swing.

4. Short Passes

As for the contact point on the foot itself, the inside of the foot offers the most control for shorter passes.

5. Long Passes

For longer passes, the top portion of the foot (where the laces are usually located) can offer a good combination of power and accuracy.

Shooting

Menu

1. Run-up

Approach the on a diagonal angle, about 4-5 steps out, with speed and a forward facing momentum.

2. Planting Foot

Your planting foot is very important. Plant your non shooting foot with great force next to the ball, at the point of impact with the ball.

3. Body Movement

Focus on a solid strong back swing and a smooth and natural forwards swing.

4. Strike

Hit the ball just below the center to avoid spin. Try to hit that ball on the hard top half of your instep. Flex your foot and lock your ankle, to achieve a strong and stable base.

5. Follow Through

Powerful straight and direct follow through.

Dribbling

Previous Next
Menu

1. Arms

When you dribble with speed move your arms as you would when you sprint without the ball.

Keep your arms out at all times when dribbling around defenders. You can use them to brush past players, improve your balance, and keep players farther away from you.

2. Athletic Stance

Center your position of gravity, bend your knees slightly, and keep your back straight (or lean forward slightly).

3. Front of Feet

Land on the front of your feet every step, whether you are dribbling forward with your laces or cutting across your body using an inside touch.

4. Footing

When you cut to change directions your place foot should hop slightly. This makes the movement quick and natural and helps you retain balance.

5. Knees

Speed and agility coaches will tell you to raise your knees high to build momentum (thus increasing speed).

Defensive Positioning

Previous Next
Menu

1. Understanding

Like decision making, position is very relative to the game. More specifically, it is very relitve to where the possession of the ball is in relation to what position you are playing.

2. Goalie

Generally the goalie should remain within the 6-yard box. However if possession is on the attacking third of the field, the goalie should push up to the top of the 18-yard box.

3. Defense

All defenders should be sure to hold a line with the center back. Moreover, the defense should shift accordingly. If the ball is on the right, the defense should shift right. This should all be done in unision..

4. Midfield

The primary objective of midfield positioning should be to deny possession through the midfield. Moreover, the midfielders should move in unision according to where the possession is on the field.

5. Attackers

The main objective of the attack is to apply pressure to the defenders. The attackers do not have much to lose as they are operating far away from their own goal, so they should aim to regain possession. Moreover, the attacker should move in unision according to where the ball in on the field.

Formations

1. Understanding Formations

Formations go from the defensive side of the field to the start. This means when reading a formation (ie. 1-4-3-3) it will start from the goalie, then defense, then midfield, then the attack.

Generally, formations will see four defenders as this creates the most ideal defense lineup.

2. 1-4-4-2

This formation is considered a standard formation. It esstially contains all neccary components (attack, defense etc.) to a good lineup, and is quite a safe option.

3. 1-4-3-3

This is also considered a very orthodox formation. However, this formation is slightly more attack centeric as it has 3 attackers. However, it still possess the regular, 4 in the defense.

4. 1-3-4-3

This formation is not very common, and is very risky. It possesses little defense (3 defenders), and is very attack heavy. You may see this formation frequently when teams need a goal.

5. 1-5-3-2

This formation is very defensive minded, as it has five defender. Thus, you may see this formation occur when teams are winning and desire to not give up any goals.

1. Understanding Decision Making

Decision making is very relative regarding the situation that the game brings. Many decisions will vary depending on the opponent, the score, and time left in a game.

2. Time

Soccer is a game where decisions need to be made quick. Do not prolong decision making as it could easily take too long; resulting in a potential loss of possession.

3. Goal

Your primary goal of each decision you make should have the end aim of allowing your team to win. Thus, you must read the sitution you are in and adjust accordingly.

4. Context

If your team is winning, there is no need to make risky decisions that could result in a loss of possession. On the contrary, if you are lossing, you may have to make more "high risk, high reward" related decisions.

5. Improvment

One of the most important aspects of decisions making involves improvement. If you make a mistake, ensure to keep this in mind for the future, so you do not make the same wrong decision.
